Background

As a shrinking city, the renewal of Detroit faces many challenges and problems like climate change and increased vacancy and poverty. Increased temperature may increase the frequency and length of extreme heat events. In Midtown, those communities with less canopy coverage, imperviousness, high-density population, and poverty could suffer more from extreme heat.

Concept

The design is focusing on climate resiliency, more access to nature, and sustainability on the abandoned sites or vacant lots. This project proposed a vacancy reuse strategy to provide a solution to these problems by designing a public vacancy lot an open tree farm/nursery, which can not only provide ecological services like street trees, shade area, and infiltration for the urban environment but also share the place for local residents to understand and enjoy the sustainable landscape. The design comes up with two main strategies to achieve the design goal:

1. Using infrastructures and terrain to support sustainable plantation and public activities;

2. Rotating plantation plan and involve public activity in the rotation to provide leisure spaces for residents and also allow residents to perceive and understand plantation dynamics.
